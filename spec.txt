Design Document: TPT Spectra
Project Name: TPT Spectra (Open-Source Medical Imaging & Diagnostics)
Industry: Healthcare & MedTech
Status: Phase 1 Design / Pre-Alpha
1. The Name
"Lumen" sounds like a lightbulb company. Spectra refers to the electromagnetic spectrum. Medical imaging isn't just one thing—it's X-rays (CT), radio waves (MRI), sound waves (Ultrasound), and gamma rays (PET). TPT Spectra covers the entire diagnostic spectrum.
2. The Mission
Your tpt-healthcare-nz manages the patient records (FHIR). TPT Spectra manages the actual multi-million-dollar machines. It is an open-source, hyper-fast, GPU-accelerated DICOM reconstruction engine written in Rust that turns raw sensor data into 3D diagnostic models in seconds, not minutes.
3. Core Components
Multi-Modal Reconstruction Engine: GPU-accelerated algorithms (using wgpu) for filtered back-projection (CT) and iterative reconstruction (MRI).
DICOM Native Parser: A zero-allocation Rust parser for the notoriously complex DICOM standard.
AI Diagnostic Bridge: A plug-and-play ONNX runtime layer so third-party AI models (tumor detection, fracture mapping) can process the 3D voxel data natively.
4. Integration with TPT Ecosystem
tpt-healthcare-nz (Patient): Stores the FHIR records and links to the imaging.
TPT Spectra (Machine): Reconstructs the scan and runs the AI.
The Link: Spectra pushes the final DICOM images and AI diagnostic confidence scores directly back into the patient's FHIR record in tpt-healthcare-nz.
Target Tech Stack: TPT Spectra (Medical Imaging)
Language: Rust (for the core reconstruction engine and DICOM parser).
GPU Libraries: wgpu or vulkan (for massively parallel image reconstruction).
DICOM Standard: dicom crate (for zero-allocation parsing of medical headers and pixel data).
AI Integration: ONNX Runtime (for running third-party diagnostic models like tumor detection directly on the voxel data).
Verification: FDA 510(k) validation tools, phantom image testing suites, and cross-referencing against the standard DCMTK (C++) library to ensure pixel-perfect parity.
